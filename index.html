About me

My name is Erica Zheng and I am a 3rd year studying data science and business with a concentration in finance, minoring in architectural design. On campus I am the director of events and marketing of Disrupt, the fintech initiative, and the director of venture acceleration of IDEA, the Northeastern's venture accelerator. Food plays a big part in my life because it is what fills me up and keeps me going throughout the day. I have two go-to meals, a mexican bowl from El Jefes, and my tried and true spam fried rice. My spam fried rice was inspired by the popular Hawaiian snack, spam musubi, which consist of a block of rice with a slice of spam glazed in a sweet, soy sauce mixture, wrapped with a piece of nori, seaweed. Once in high school, I was too lazy to put it together, and decided to make a "deconstructed" spam musubi. I added my own touch to it, adding egg, and instead of a piece of nori, I grabbed a pack of snacked seaweed. 4 years later, it is not only my go-to meal in college but a highly requested meal by all my friends.
